# Horn Clause

A Horn clause is disjunctions of literals s.t. **at most one** literal is **positive**. It could also be written in implicative form which is commonly used in [[形式化方法|Formal Methods]].

- Exactly one positive literal:  definite clause (strict Horn clause)
$$p \land q \land \ldots \rightarrow t$$
- No positive literal: goal clause
$$p \land q \land \ldots \rightarrow \mathtt{false}$$ 

And a *fact* is when there's no negative literal:

$$
\mathtt{true} \rightarrow t
$$
