大众都相信的 7 个德鲁伊谣言 
7 Myths Everyone Believes About Druids

---

Listen, you've got it all wrong.
听着，你们全都搞错了。

---

Frequently, the picture you see of a druid is some green-haired boy scout cavorting atop a white wolf, or gazing soulfully into the eyes of a moose.
通常，你所看到的德鲁伊画面都是一帮绿头发的童子军在白狼身上嬉戏，或者深情地凝视驼鹿的眼睛。

---

They're depicted as serene bringers of balance and peace, who live in harmony with all life.
他们都被描绘成平衡与和睦的宁静使者，与所有生命和谐相处。

---

Nope. No. Nein. Druids are creatures possessed by the cold spirit of Nature.
不对，不对，内因，德鲁伊是大自然冷酷精神附身的生物。

---

Druids love natural spaces. It's even fair to say that druids feed on them.
德鲁伊喜欢大自然的空间，甚至可以公平地说，德鲁伊以其为食。

---

They feed on the snow that falls on ancient pines. They feed on the weevils gnawing on roots. They feed on the hunger in a wolf's belly.
他们以古松上的落雪为食，以啃咬根部的象鼻虫为食，以狼腹中的饥饿为食。

---

Because these things are Natural, they are Good, for at least one definition of "Good".
因为这些东西属于大自然，因此就是好的——至少符合“好”的一个定义。

---

Then what is Bad, in the eyes of a druid?
那么，在德鲁伊眼中，什么是“坏”的呢。

---

Why, all the things that destroy the natural order. Rationality, math (druids hate math), language, money, metallurgy, fur trappers, philosophy. The philosophy of a druid is "no philosophy".
为什么所有破坏自然秩序的东西：理性、数学（德鲁伊讨厌数学）、语言、金钱、冶炼、毛皮猎人、哲学（德鲁伊的哲学就是“没有哲学”）都在此列？

---

And true, druids use language to talk among themselves, but it is always tinged with a bit of self-loathing for this reason, a reminder of their distance from Nature and from what is Good.
诚然，德鲁伊会用语言来互相交谈，但这也是他们总是带有一点自我厌恶色彩的原因——这会提醒他们与自然以及“好”之间的距离。

---

Nature is red in tooth in claw. Nature is hungry and rapacious. Nature is self-absorbed.
大自然是尖牙利爪上的猩红，大自然是饥饿和贪婪，大自然是固执己见。

---

Does a panther care about anything beyond it's own well-being?
难道豹子会关心自己幸福外的其他事情吗？

---

And because these things are Nature, they are also Druids.
因为这些东西都是大自然，所以它们也全是德鲁伊。

---

Every druid's dream in life is to become a giant grizzly bear, fat and unchallenged.
每个德鲁伊一生的梦想都是成为一只巨大的灰熊，肥肥胖胖，所向无敌。

---

Myth #1 - Druids hate fire.
谣言 1：德鲁伊讨厌火 Myth #1 - Druids hate fire

---

Druids love fire.
德鲁伊喜欢火。

---

There is no better way to raze a town than to burn it.
夷平整个城镇最好的方式莫过于将其烧毁。

---

Fire is the great equalizer.
火是伟大的平衡造就者。

---

Druids love a dry summer storm. Thatched roofs ignite so readily under lightning strikes, like they've been waiting for the flame.
德鲁伊喜欢干燥的夏日风暴，草屋顶在雷击下很容易引燃，仿佛它们一直渴望着烈焰。

---

Druids call to the winds, and the winds call back, blowing the inferno before them, across the town. Forest predators pad through the streets on quick paws.
德鲁伊会呼唤风，风也会发起回应，将他们眼前的火狱吹过城镇，森林掠食者靠着锋利的爪子在街道上穿行。

---

Farther back in the smoke, you can see the naked, blood-painted druids moving from house to house, methodically exterminating dogs.
在烟雾后方更远处，你能看到赤身裸体，涂满鲜血的德鲁伊从一家走到另一家，有条不紊地消灭所有的狗。

---

Druids hate domesticated dogs.
德鲁伊讨厌驯化的狗。

---

Aside from being able to track them back into their secret caves in the forest, dogs represent a traitorous element among the animals.
狗不光能追踪他们回到森林中的秘密洞窟，还是动物之中的叛徒。

---

Domesticated dogs are wolves that have been twisted so thoroughly in the hands of their masters that they hold nothing but love for their oppressors.
狼在主人的手中彻底扭曲成了驯化的狗，除了对压迫者的敬爱外别无他物。

---

Dogs are an abomination on par with the undead.
狗是和不死族同级的可憎之物。

---

Myth #2 - Druids hate to see a tree burn.
谣言 2：德鲁伊讨厌看到树被烧毁 Myth #2 - Druids hate to see a tree burn

---

Druids don't care about a tree. Druids care about the forest.
德鲁伊才不在乎一棵树，德鲁伊关心的是整片森林。

---

And not even the forest-as-a-collection-of-trees, they care about the eternal forest, the forest that will exist long after all of these trees are dead, and also somehow exists behind them.
甚至不是树木组成的森林，而是关心永恒的森林，是即使所有树木都已经凋零乃至德鲁伊自己撒手人寰后还能长久存在的森林。

---

The template from which this forest grew.
这是森林生长的模板。

---

The pulse of Nature is one of death and rebirth, twined together as tightly as two anacondas fucking.
大自然的脉搏就是死亡与重生的脉搏，两者像水蚺一样紧紧缠绕交配。

---

An animal is born, and is promptly eaten. Another animal is born, eats and grows, procreates, then grows old and is eaten.
动物出生后被迅速吃掉，另一只动物出生，进食，成长，繁衍后代，变老，然后被吃掉。

---

Chaos and apathy. This is Right and this is Good.
混乱和冷漠，这是对的，这是好的。

---

Forests burn. This is part of their cycle.
森林燃烧也只是循环的一部分。

---

Take a break from this blog to read about wildfire ecology.
先不用继续看这个博客，去读读关于野火的生态。

---

I'll be here when you get back.
我会在这里等你回来。

---

Druids bring balance wherever they go, but it is rarely the sort of balance that you are expecting.
德鲁伊无论走到哪里都会带来平衡，但很少是你所期望的平衡。

---

What's more abhorrent to Nature? A forest fire or farmland?
森林大火和农田，试问那个对于大自然而言更加可恶？

---

Civilized folk must be very careful when using forest fires to fight druids.
文明人在利用森林大火对付德鲁伊时必须特别小心。

---

Fires are useful because they deprive druids of food and animal resources.
火很有用，因为其能剥夺德鲁伊的食物和动物资源。

---

But on the other hand, fire is one of a druid's elements. Other things that druids can bend to their will: wind, rain, nitrogen fixation.
但另一方面，火也是德鲁伊的元素之一，和风、雨、固氮一样都是德鲁伊能用意志加以操纵的东西。

---

Those who would use forest fires against the druids often live just long enough to see their forest fire turned against them.
用森林大火对付德鲁伊的家伙通常足以活到目睹自己遭到大火反噬的那一刻。

---

Druids will sometimes set forest fires themselves.
德鲁伊有时也会引燃森林大火。

---

It's their greatest resource when a king sends an army into the forest after them, as kings invariably do.
当国王派出军队（这些人总是这样）追着他们进到森林时，这就是德鲁伊手上最厉害的武器。

---

Druids guide the wildfire, and sometimes even extinguish it after it has done its job.
德鲁伊会引导野火，有时甚至会在野火完成任务后将其扑灭。

---

A druid's magic is broad, crude, slow, indistinct, subtle, and immensely powerful.
德鲁伊的魔法广泛、粗糙、缓慢、模糊、隐晦且极其强大。

---

Compare this to a wizard's magic, which is fast, localized, instantaneous, precise, and often flashy.
相比之下，法师的魔法则是快速、局部、瞬间、精确并且通常很华丽。

---

A wizard's magic is a bullet fired from a gun, while a druid's magic is a gentle, sustained breeze.
法师的魔法是从枪中射出的子弹，而德鲁伊的魔法则是温和而持续的微风。

---

But which of these two things requires more joules of energy?
但是试问这两者哪个需要更多能量？

---

Myth #3 - Druids exist in harmony with all living things.
谣言 3：德鲁伊能和所有生命和谐共存 Myth #3 - Druids exist in harmony with all living things

---

Druids can barely live in harmony with each other.
德鲁伊几乎无法和彼此和谐相处。

---

These people are possessed by spirits of Nature, figuratively but also possibly literally.
这些人遭到自然之灵附身——这既可能是象征性，也可能是实质性。

---

When one commune meets another, it is like two wolf packs catching sight of each other across the timber line.
当两个公社之间彼此相遇时，就和两群狼在林线对面看到了对方一样。

---

Sometimes there is murder. Sometimes there are "marriages". Sometimes they exchange small bits of news and go on their way.
有时会发生残杀，有时候会有“联姻”，有时候他们会交换一下消息，然后各走各路。

---

Even within a stable circle of druids there is tension.
即使在稳定的德鲁伊圈子里面也和狼群一样存在紧张关系。

---

Like wolf packs, druids have their alphas and their betas. They have their breeding pairs and their marginalized members.
德鲁伊也有自己首领和副手，有自己的繁殖配偶和边缘化成员。

---

Druidic power is invested in them in proportion to their standing, so advancing through the ranks of the druids involves killing your superiors (and usually eating them--druids are frugal).
德鲁伊的力量则是根据其地位成比例地提升，因此要在德鲁伊的行列中晋升，你就得杀死自己的上级（通常还要吃掉对方——德鲁伊不会浪费）。

---

This is what the average person knows about druids: They are wild people who live in forests who will murder you on sight. Also, they're magic.
这就是普通人对德鲁伊的了解：这帮野人生活在森林当中，一见面就杀过来，并且还会魔法。

---

And for most people, that's the beginning and the end of it.
对于大多数人而言，这就是全部了。

---

Forests are wild, untamed places.
森林是未经开垦的荒野之地。

---

On Earth, humans venture into them to go hunting or collect mushrooms. On Centerra, no sane person would go into a forest--any forest--because of the druids (and various beasties).
在地球上，人类会冒险深入去打猎或是采集蘑菇，而在赛特拉这里，没有一个脑子清醒的人会进入森林——任何森林，因为这里有德鲁伊（和各种猛兽）。

---

Nobles go hunting in carefully tended game preserves, but these are small and semi-analogous to golf courses.
贵族会在精心打点的猎物保护区里面打猎，但这些保护区都很小，类似高尔夫球场。

---

Sometimes the druids do not have the power to assert complete dominion over their forests, and so are forced to let travelers through. Even hunters might be tolerated (with occasional murders to keep them feeling unwelcome, but not so many that it brings armies and wizards down on their heads).
有时候德鲁伊没有足够力量去统治自己所在的整片森林，因此被迫放任旅者通行，乃至猎人有时候也会得到容忍——虽然偶尔也要杀上几个，让对方知道这里不欢迎自己，但也不会干得太过火，避免军队和法师找上门来。

---

Druids hate elves only slightly less than the rest of civilization.
德鲁伊对于精灵的憎恶稍逊于其他文明。

---

True, elves have a very small ecological footprint, but they also build houses and wear shoes.
诚然，精灵的生态足迹非常小，但他们也会建造房屋和穿鞋。

---

And besides, elves have a lot of powerful spellcasters.
此外，精灵还有许多强大的法术使。

---

Mostly elves and druids try to pretend that the other one doesn't exist.
所以大部分精灵和德鲁伊都试图无视对方。

---

When elves and druids do fight, it's godawful.
若是精灵和德鲁伊打了起来，那就真的是惊天地泣鬼神。

---

Weeping treants marching solemnly into a bonfire, epileptic wolves gnawing off their own legs, that sort of thing.
哭泣的树人会庄重地走进篝火堆中，癫痫的狼啃掉自己的腿部，诸如此类。

---

Myth #4 - Druids want cities and forests to coexist in harmony.
谣言 4：德鲁伊希望城市和森林和谐相处 Myth #4 - Druids want cities and forests to coexist in harmony

---

Druids hate cities. They hate the concept of cities.
德鲁伊憎恨城市，他们讨厌城市的概念。

---

Sometimes, when a trapper is filling his canteen by a creek and a druid drops out of the trees--eyes wide and teeth bared--and proceeds to murder the trapper with a sharpened rock, the trapper dies thinking that the druid hates him.
有时，当捕猎者在小溪边上用水壶装水时，某个德鲁伊从树上跳下——怒目圆睁、呲牙咧嘴——然后开始用锋利的石块杀死捕猎者。

---

But that's not true. The druid looks right through him and sees the city.
捕猎者临死前会以为德鲁伊憎恨自己，但事实并非如此，而是德鲁伊透过对方看到了城市。

---

When the druid bites out the trapper's jugular vein, the druid can taste the dirty rainwater dripping off the gargoyles. When the druid pulls out the trapper's heart and eats it, he can feel concrete foundations crumbling between his molars.
当德鲁伊咬断捕猎者的颈静脉时，他可以尝到从石像鬼雕塑上滴落肮脏雨水的味道；当德鲁伊撕出捕猎者的心脏并将其吃掉时，他能感觉到臼齿之间的混凝土地基正在崩塌。

---

He didn't kill a man, he killed a piece of the city.
他没有杀人，而是杀死了城市的一部分。

---

Because the druid knows that the Forest is not just a collection of trees.
因为德鲁伊知道森林不仅仅是树木的集合，而是一处灵境。

---

It is a spiritual place that everything will return to, if given enough time.
只要有足够的时间，一切都会回归其中。

---

And like the Forest, the City is also a spiritual place, greater than the sum of its cobbles.
就和森林一样，城市也是一处灵境，比里面的鹅卵石加起来还要伟大。

---

But the City doesn't come from the World.
但城市并非来自世界，城市来自于人类的思想。

---

The City comes from the mind of Men, and that is why the druid takes extra pleasure in smashing the trapper's brains out with his rock.
这就是为什么德鲁伊特别喜欢用石头砸碎捕猎者的大脑。

---

If the druid had any sense of symbolism, he would eschew the process of eating the trapper's brains.
若是德鲁伊能理解符号象征，他就不会吃掉捕猎者的大脑。

---

But symbols are profane, another tool of the City, and so the brains are carefully scooped up with dirty fingers and swiftly eaten.
但亵渎的符号象征又是城市的另一种工具，所以他会用脏兮兮的手指将大脑舀起来迅速吃掉。

---

Myth #5 - Druids don't destroy cities.
谣言 5：德鲁伊不会摧毁城市 Myth #5 - Druids don't destroy cities

---

Let me tell you two stories. Here is the first one.
让我给大家讲两个故事，这是第一个。

---

During the Time of Fire and Madness, the elves were safe in their tree, which was called Eladras.
在「火焰与狂乱纪元」，精灵安全地留在称为埃拉德拉斯的大树上。

---

They were safe because the Great Tree did not touch the ground.
因为大树还没接触地面，所以他们很安全。

---

It's roots were in the moon, and it's branches grew downwards into the atmosphere, and brushed the tips of the highest mountains.
其根系在月亮上，枝条往下生长到大气层中，拂过最高山峰的顶部，这是精灵的栖息之地。

---

This is where the elves lived, and because they were safe from that great tragedy, they were able to preserve a great deal of knowledge and tools from What Came Before.
由于他们安全地躲过了这次大悲剧，所以保存了大量来自「前世」的知识和工具。

---

But the Great Tree was broken in 511 TFM, and fell to the earth among the knuckles of the Elterspine Mountains, where it died.
但这棵大树在 511 TFM 时折断，倒在埃尔特斯派恩山脉的山脊之中，并在此死去。

---

Here is the second story.
这是第二个故事。

---

The druids of the Kerwood conspired with the dragons of that place to put an end to the cities. (Wild dragons--as opposed to civilized dragons--have deep and profound reasons to despise cities, but those are outside the scope of this retelling.)
克伍德的德鲁伊和此地的龙合谋要消灭城市（野蛮龙和文明龙相反，它们有深刻而重大的理由鄙视城市，但这超出了本文所述的范畴）。

---

They called themselves Roa Junyo, which in the old tongue means "Confederacy of Beasts". They planted seven seeds together. Oak, ash, ivy, thistle, mandrake, dandelion, and a seed from the Great Tree, Eladras.
这些家伙自称洛阿·纯阳（在古语的意思中是“野兽联盟”），并一起种下了七颗种子：橡树、白蜡树、常春藤、蓟、曼德拉草、蒲公英和大树埃拉德拉斯的种子。

---

(Fuck if I know where they found it.)
（谁他妈知道他们从哪里搞到这个），

---

The tree that grew from this union was called Aglabendis, the Second World Tree.
从这个结合中长出来的树称为阿拉本迪斯，即第二世界树。

---

It grew a foot a day for a hundred years, and Roa Junyo used it to wage war on the world.
其百年来每天长一英尺，洛阿·纯阳则以之向全世界发动战争。

---

It sent roots throughout the entire world. Castles crumbled under its patient predation. City walls were ground to dust over the course of a year.
它的根须遍布整个世界，城堡在其耐心的啃食下轰然倒塌，城墙在一年之内被磨成尘土。

---

Millions were driven from their farms into the wilderness, where most of them starved.
数百万的人被赶出农场跑进荒野，大部分人挨饿受饥。

---

For a hundred years, Aglabendis was implacable.
这一百年内，阿拉本迪斯毫不停歇。

---

Dragons and druids torched the great cities, while Aglabendis' heavy roots mutilated their bones.
龙类和德鲁伊烧毁了各大城市，阿拉本迪斯则碾碎了后者的骨骼。

---

The Great Library of Asria was put to the torch. The Immortal Palace of the Peacock Emperor was shoved into the sea.
阿斯里亚大图书馆被付之一炬，孔雀大帝的不朽仙宫被铲入海中。

---

Dragons landed in the great cattle markets of Kelzon and glutted themselves on the cows found there, then laid themselves down to sleep amid the mud and the blood and the gore, while the few remaining cows kicked at the paddock gates for a way out. Crown jewels were captured and melted down.
飞龙降落在凯尔松的牲畜大市场，狼吞虎咽地吃下找到的奶牛。 然后在泥泞、鲜血和内脏中躺下睡觉，剩下几头奶牛则踢着围场大门寻求出路。

---

The gold was mixed with pig iron, and the resultant slag dumped over the deep parts of the ocean. Paintings were smeared with feces and thrown into the wells, which were then collapsed. (In fact, this is where the famous Portrait of a Pale Girl was recovered.) All farm animals were killed and eaten, and what could not be eaten (most of it) was left to rot. Pearls were fed to swine.
皇冠上的珠宝被撕下融化，黄金和生铁融合后的炉渣被倒在大洋深处，画作被涂上粪便然后扔到井中，接着井也被随之推倒（事实上，著名的《苍白之女肖像》就是在这里重见天日），所有农场动物都被杀死后吃掉，（大部分）吃不掉的就任其腐烂，珍珠也被喂给了猪吃。

---

Roa Junyo was a terrible foe. They did not want anything that the cities could offer, just destruction.
洛阿·纯阳乃是可怕大敌，他们不想要城市提供的任何东西，只想着把一切化为乌有。

---

No normal spies could infiltrate their ranks. Men would rather have fought orcs than the naked ranks of the druids.
普通的间谍没有一个能混进这帮家伙当中，人们宁可与兽人战斗也不愿意对上赤身裸体的德鲁伊战列。

---

In the height of Aglabendis' power, the tree used its roots to conduct raids.
在阿拉本迪斯的力量最为鼎盛的时候，这棵树会利用根部发起袭击。

---

After spending months growing beneath a fort or a town, they would suddenly erupt from the ground, crushing wagons beneath their bulk.
根部会在堡垒或是城镇地下生长数个月后猛然从地面喷发而出，压碎枝干下的马车。

---

They were also usable as tunnels, and so the dire roots also vomited out bears, wolf packs, and wasps. (Cities quickly came to respect the power and utility of few million wasps during a raid.)

如果你希望在游戏里面的德鲁伊都是快乐的嬉皮士，那很棒。

希望上面的段落能提供一些关于德鲁伊如何成为有趣坏蛋的思路。

我也试着不光把德鲁伊当成角色职业，而是在此之上给予其一点文化。

背景很重要，德鲁伊则是有非常好的引子。
